About

Ever heard of Muzic in your life?

Well now you have! We are a dynamic platform celebrating music in all its forms. Discover new talents, explore diverse genres, and enjoy high-quality audio, curated playlists, and livestreamed performances.

Every day, our platform features a "Showcase of the Day", highlighting exceptional artists who are making waves in the music scene. Additionally, we present an "Annual Picks" list to spotlight the three most popular and influential musicians of the year.

Join us in supporting artists from around the globe, and find Muzic for you!

Weekly Spotlight Channel

Jump into each of these selections below! They are compiled from past showcases of the day

Check these out every week!

Frank Ocean's "Pyramids" from his 2012 album "Channel Orange" is a musical journey that beautifully combines R&B, soul, and electronic elements. Clocking in at nearly 10 minutes, the song's intricate production, ranging from smooth synths to funky guitar riffs, creates an immersive experience. With its vivid storytelling and Ocean's soulful vocals, "Pyramids" stands out as a modern masterpiece, offering a unique blend of nostalgia and futurism that transcends genre boundaries.

Isabelle Huang's "Itch" is a mesmerizing Mandopop track that beautifully captures romantic longing. Huang’s soulful vocals, combined with the song’s lush and energetic composition, create a unique blend that leaves a lasting impression. The lyrics, which depict a gentle beam of a setting sun and a sea of blue bright sky, are a testament to Huang’s musical versatility and evocative singing ability.

“Hello?” by Clairo and Reggie Snow is a captivating track that beautifully explores the complexities of a romance strained by physical and emotional separation. The song’s self-produced instrumental, featuring a simple two-chord synth loop and a bouncy drum beat, is reminiscent of Clairo’s previous SoundCloud hit "Flaming Hot Cheetos". Clairo’s tender vocals paired with Reggie Snow’s reflective verses create a unique blend of indie pop and lo-fi alternative pop that leaves a lasting impression

"Heartbeat, Heartbreak" is a background song in Persona 4, sung by Shihoko Hirata. It plays during cloudy and light rainy days in the game. The lyrics narrate the subject's shattered dreams and broken promises, yet their persistent heartbeat symbolizes resilience and the determination to move forward amidst emotional turmoil. The repetitive chorus of “Heartbeat, heartbeat, it keeps on pounding. Heartbreak, heartbreak, you tell me goodbye" deeply impacts avid enjoyers of the Persona 4 with its underlying themes of betrayal within the game's storyline.

“Borombon” by Camilo Azuquita is a classic Latin song from 1969. It’s a vibrant fusion of Latin sounds, cumbia, tropical and calypso funk. The song has been well received by fans, reflecting its nostalgic factor and popularity. Azuquita’s powerful voice, striking trumpets, and rich piano create an energetic atmosphere. Most popularly, this song was featured in the show "Better Call Saul" during a car scene with the major character Lalo Salamanca. Whether you’re a fan of Latin music or just discovering it, “Borombon” is a song worth adding to your playlist.

"Palace/Curse" by The Internet, from their Ego Death album, is a unique story of two contrasting approaches to attracting a girl's attention, showcasing both a playful and an alluring approach. The song, featuring Tyler, The Creator and Steve Lacy, humorously depicts a house party scenario in the first half, with Syd and Tyler persuading a girl to return to their places. The second half explores the captivating trap of love set by the girl. The song leaves a lasting impression with its mish-mash of initially upbeat vocals with a slower, lush composition in the later parts.

Future Goals

Cross-App Integrations

We would like to develop a unified music discovery feature that allows users to pull song recommendations from all linked music platforms like Spotify and SoundCloud, providing users with a diverse range of tailored music suggestions to create a seamless music listening experience. It would also give users the flexibility to mix and match songs, expanding their music discovery possibilities.

Expanding Community Features

We also would like to create a feature that allows artists to share updates, behind-the-scenes content, and interact with fans directly on the app. This could include live chats during performances, Q&A sessions, or even virtual meet-and-greets. There are plans in the works to make a social media app (MUZE) that would allow users to interact with one another as an interwoven community.

Multiplatform Accessibility

Lastly, we aim to expand the app to other platforms such as iOS, Android, and smart TVs to reach a wider audience (coming soon with iFrames). Additionally, we want to implement an offline mode that allows users to download songs and listen to them without an internet connection, making the app more accessible and convenient for users with limited or intermittent internet access.
